About Palakkad

Palakkad, known as the Gateway to Kerala, is a land of lush paddy fields, gentle hills, and vibrant cultural traditions. Located at the foothills of the Western Ghats, it is famous for the Palakkad Gap, a natural mountain pass that influences the region’s climate and biodiversity. The district is rich in rivers, plantations, and scenic landscapes, making it an agriculturally prosperous area, often called the “rice bowl of Kerala.” Beyond its natural beauty, Palakkad is home to a unique blend of rural charm and town vibrancy, where traditional festivals, music, dance forms like Kathakali, and local crafts form an integral part of daily life. With attractions such as Palakkad Fort, Malampuzha Gardens, Nelliampathi Hills, Silent Valley National Park, and serene villages, the district offers travelers a harmonious experience of nature, culture, and authentic Kerala lifestyle.
